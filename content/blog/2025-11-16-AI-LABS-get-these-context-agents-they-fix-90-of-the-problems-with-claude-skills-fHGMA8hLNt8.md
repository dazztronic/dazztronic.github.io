- "Claude Skills" are defined as modular, reusable context agents that encapsulate assets, references, and logic for a specific task, enabling Claude to perform specialized automation far beyond generic prompting.

- "Skill references" are the curated set of files, templates, and scripts that provide the necessary background for the skill to operate effectively.

### What You Should Do
- Identify 1-2 repetitive tasks in your workflow and prototype a Claude Skill using assets and references specific to those tasks.
- Leverage the provided npm package to bootstrap your own skill library, then incrementally customize for your unique needs.

### What to Avoid
- Relying solely on generic prompts leads to inconsistent results and missed automation opportunities‚Äîinvest in skill setup for repeatability.
- Failing to provide comprehensive references or assets to a skill will limit its effectiveness and may produce incomplete outputs.
- Overloading a single skill with too many responsibilities can create maintenance headaches; keep skills focused and modular.

### Best Practices
- Package each skill with clear, versioned references and scripts to ensure reproducibility and easy updates.
- Organize outputs (e.g., social media posts, test reports) in structured folders with naming conventions for easy retrieval and auditing.
- Iteratively refine skills based on feedback and evolving workflow requirements, treating them as living automation assets.
