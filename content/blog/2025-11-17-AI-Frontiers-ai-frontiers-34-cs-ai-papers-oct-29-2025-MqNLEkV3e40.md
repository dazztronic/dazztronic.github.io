### Concepts & Definitions
- Agentic AI: Systems that autonomously plan and act, functioning as independent agents capable of complex, adaptive behavior.
- Reinforcement learning: A paradigm where AI learns optimal actions through trial and error, guided by rewards in simulated or real environments.
- Trustworthiness: A composite measure of an AI model's truthfulness, robustness to adversarial prompts, and ethical safety, especially in sensitive domains.
- Zero-shot learning: The ability of a model to generalize to new tasks or domains without additional fine-tuning.

### What You Should Do
- Consider hybridizing small, reasoning-focused models with larger LLMs to maximize cost-effectiveness and reliability.
- Benchmark AI systems in domain-specific, adversarial contexts to uncover trust and ethics gaps before deployment.
- Implement parallel tool use and modular agentic frameworks to accelerate agent execution in complex workflows.

### What to Avoid
- Relying solely on general-purpose LLMs can mask critical trust and ethics failures in specialized domains (e.g., biosecurity).
- Neglecting energy and cost optimization can limit the scalability and accessibility of advanced agentic AI.
- Overfitting agentic systems to narrow benchmarks may undermine real-world robustness and adaptability.

### Best Practices
- Use modular, agentic frameworks (dual loops, parallel graphs) to structure complex AI planning and execution.
