## Overview

Sadie St. Lawrence frames 2023 as a pivotal year for treating data as a product, emphasizing enterprise-level opportunities to monetize unique data assets and predicting a surge in multimodal generative AI, especially in video. She spotlights the practical and ethical dimensions of automation, the rise of consumer-facing AI, and the need for sustainable compute in the march toward AGI. Her perspective is actionable for both practitioners and business leaders seeking to leverage proprietary data and anticipate the next wave of AI-driven products.

## üîç Key Insights & Learnings

### Creator's Unique Angle
St. Lawrence uniquely positions 'data as a product' not just as a technical or consumer trend, but as a strategic enterprise imperative‚Äîurging companies to mine their own unique datasets for new lines of business, as exemplified by Tesla's insurance model. She also forecasts the next leap in generative AI will be multimodal, with compelling video generation as the next frontier, and grounds her predictions in real-world productization and enterprise adoption rather than just research breakthroughs.

### The Core Problem
Organizations struggle to differentiate in an increasingly crowded AI landscape, often defaulting to generic tools or datasets, and risk missing out on unique value creation. Simultaneously, the field faces challenges around the social impact of automation, data bias, and the sustainability of compute as models scale toward AGI.

### The Solution Approach
St. Lawrence advocates for a dual approach: (1) Treat internal, proprietary data as a core product asset‚Äîidentify, package, and monetize it in new business models (e.g., Tesla's use of driver data for insurance). (2) Stay ahead of the curve by watching and experimenting with multimodal AI, particularly in video, and by leveraging consumer-facing tools as inspiration for enterprise innovation. She emphasizes the importance of data literacy, ethical frameworks, and sustainable compute strategies.

### Key Insights
- Enterprise value will increasingly come from unique, proprietary data products, not just from adopting off-the-shelf AI tools.
- The next major generative AI leap is likely to be in multimodal models, especially video, combining text, image, and audio inputs for richer outputs.
- Consumer-facing AI applications (like Lensa and ChatGPT) are setting expectations and opening new markets, which enterprises can learn from.
- Ethical and social impacts of automation are now mainstream concerns, with conferences and regulations (e.g., AI Bill of Rights) requiring explicit attention.
- Sustainable compute is a looming challenge‚Äîpractitioners must innovate to do more with less data and processing power.

### Concepts & Definitions
- "Data as a product": Framed as the practice of treating internal datasets as core business assets, capable of being packaged, monetized, or spun into new business lines.
- "Multimodal models": Defined as AI systems that process and generate multiple types of data (e.g., text, images, video, audio) in a unified workflow.
- "AutoML": Explained as tools that automate the optimization and deployment of machine learning models, with both productivity gains and social risks.

### Technical Details & Implementation
- Tesla's insurance product leverages granular driver data to build superior risk models, demonstrating a workflow where enterprise data is directly monetized.
- Multimodal AI architectures are highlighted‚Äîcombining LLMs (like GPT-3/4), image generators (DALL-E 2), and audio tools to create richer, more interactive outputs.
- Consumer tools like Lensa and ChatGPT are cited as examples of packaging complex AI for intuitive user experiences, a model for enterprise productization.

### Contrarian Takes & Different Approaches
- Challenges the notion that only consumer-facing AI startups can innovate‚Äîargues that enterprises with unique data can outcompete by creating differentiated products.
- Pushes back on the hype that AGI is imminent, highlighting the need for sustainable compute and more efficient models rather than just scaling up.
